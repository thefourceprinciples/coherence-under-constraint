# Appendix B — Cross-Disciplinary Evidence Review

---

## B1. Purpose

This appendix surveys empirical and theoretical literature demonstrating that:

- coherence  
- synchronization  
- critical transitions  
- constraint-shaped dynamics  
- interface-dominated behavior  

occur across multiple scientific domains.

It does **not** claim that Coherence Under Constraint (CUC) is already proven as a universal law.

Instead:

> CUC is presented as a unifying interpretive layer over established domain-specific findings.

---

## B2. Evidence Logic

The literature clusters around five recurring motifs:

1. Phase synchronization  
2. Nonequilibrium self-organization  
3. Attractors and critical transitions  
4. Network-mediated coordination  
5. Interface / boundary dominance  

These appear across:

- physics  
- biology  
- neuroscience  
- ecology  
- financial systems  

---

## B3. Physics and Nonequilibrium Order

### Self-Organization

Prigogine’s work demonstrated that:

- far-from-equilibrium systems can generate order  
- irreversible processes can create structure  

---

### Synchronization

Kuramoto-style models show:

- coherence emerges from interacting oscillators  
- synchronization has measurable stability conditions  

---

### Macroscopic Coherence

Examples:

- Bose–Einstein condensates  
- superconductivity  

These systems exhibit:

- large-scale phase coherence  
- constraint-dependent stability  

---

## B4. Biology and Distributed Coordination

### Biofilm Synchronization

Studies show:

- bacterial communities exhibit synchronized oscillations  
- long-range signaling coordinates distant cells  

Key insight:

> coherence is not limited to cognition—it exists at microbial scales  

---

### Development and Morphogenesis

Biological form emerges from:

- signaling gradients  
- constrained interaction networks  

CUC interpretation:

structure = coherence basin under constraint

---

## B5. Neuroscience and Coherence

### Communication Through Coherence

Fries (2005):

- neural communication depends on phase alignment  

---

### Large-Scale Synchronization

Hipp, Engel, Siegel:

- coherence predicts perception  

Salazar et al.:

- task-specific synchronization supports cognition  

---

### Interpretation

Coherence is:

- measurable  
- functional  
- central to neural integration  

---

## B6. Ecology and Critical Transitions

### Early Warning Signals

Dakos & Bascompte:

- critical slowing down predicts collapse  

Chen et al.:

- experimental ecosystems show transition precursors  

---

### Implication

Ecosystems exhibit:

- basin stability  
- measurable transition signatures  

---

## B7. Economic and Network Systems

### Market Synchronization

Observations:

- asset correlations increase during crises  
- volatility clusters  

---

### Network Contagion

Studies show:

- shared exposures amplify risk  
- network topology governs propagation  

---

### Interpretation

Markets display:

- coherence buildup  
- abrupt regime shifts  

---

## B8. Interface and Boundary Effects

Across domains, boundaries amplify dynamics:

- neural coupling across regions  
- biofilm signaling interfaces  
- ecological trophic boundaries  
- financial network overlaps  

CUC framing:

> interfaces are high-impact coherence zones  

---

## B9. Limits and Negative Evidence

A rigorous framework must acknowledge limitations:

- early-warning signals are not universally reliable  
- correlation ≠ meaningful coherence  
- synchronization can reflect instability  

Therefore:

> CUC should be framed as a research program, not a closed theory  

---

## B10. Synthesis

Across domains:

- coherence is measurable  
- constraints shape dynamics  
- transitions show precursor signals  
- network structure influences outcomes  

Conclusion:

> A unifying structural framework is scientifically reasonable
