# Part IV — Experimental Tests and Empirical Research Program

---

## 40. The Empirical Challenge

A unified systems framework must ultimately be evaluated through empirical observation.

For the CUC framework, the central question is:

**Do systems across different domains exhibit measurable coherence dynamics governed by constraint architecture?**

Testing requires:

- measurable coherence metrics  
- identifiable system constraints  
- observable transitions between coherent and incoherent states  

The objective is to determine whether common mathematical patterns emerge across disciplines.

---

## 41. Measuring Coherence

Coherence can be quantified using multiple approaches depending on domain.

### Phase Synchronization Metrics
- Phase Locking Value (PLV)  
- Kuramoto Order Parameter  

Used in physics and neuroscience to measure oscillatory alignment.

---

### Information-Based Metrics
- Mutual Information  
- Transfer Entropy  

Measure coordinated behavior between components.

---

### Correlation Structures
Large-scale systems exhibit coherence through correlation:

- financial market correlations  
- ecological population cycles  
- neural activity patterns
